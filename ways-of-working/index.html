Transformation Team Vision

The way we work: We work in multi-discipliary teams, helping non-digital teams to transform services using data-driven insights and user-centred digital solutions. We deliver value early, safely and iteratively rather than in a big bang. We believe in doing the right thing over following a rigid process (except for safety and governance). We only make promises we can keep, and meet our own deadlines, or quickly say when we cannot.

Do

  • follow this guidance for all projects you work on
  • tailor this advice for your project, unless stated otherwise
  • ask colleagues, and make adjustments to this guidance, if anything is unclear

Don't

  • do not tailor any processes related to information governance, digital clinical safety, governance or contracts
  • do not create a new project artefact unless it is absolutely needed (minimum viable bureaucracy rule)

How we govern our work

These processes are followed across the whole directorate and should not be adapted as they make sure we spend public money well and meet our legal obligations to keep people, and their information, safe.

  • Starting a new project / phase

    Guidance on the processes that must be followed when starting a new project or phase, such as service standard assessments.

  • Ensuring clinical safety

    How we manage clinical risk during digital transformation, following DCB0129 or DCB0160 frameworks.

  • Keeping data safe

    How we keep people's data safe and secure, following Information Governance best practice.

How we deliver projects (delivery management)

The heart of what we do is making stuff happen. We give lots of freedom to project managers to decide what works for their project, but provide guidance on what good delivery management looks like as well as a few of our non-negotiable project rituals.

  • Adhering to standards

    Guidance on the standards that must be followed when delivering any project within BOB (i.e. NHS Service Standard).

  • Using Agile

    How we use the Agile frameworks, Scrum and Kanban to deliver most projects.

  • Using Waterfall (by exception)

    Guidance on when it's acceptable to use Waterfall project management and how we use PRINCE2 to deliver projects in this way.

  • Non-negotiables

    The set of processes and rituals we always follow for kick-off, reflection and wellbeing during projects (including templates).
