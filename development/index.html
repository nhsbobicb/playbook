The way we learn and develop: As a team, learning and developing together and as individuals is very important. We dedicate the right amount of time and effort and support one another to achieve personal and team goals. We develop skills to let us progress in our roles now, as well as continue our future career paths.

All of our learning and development offer is tied to the national Federation for Informatics Professionals (FEDIP) job families. This divides skills into five levels, each associted with an AfC banding.

  • Associate Practitioner (Band 2/3/4)
  • Practitioner (Band 5/6)
  • Senior Practitioner (Band 7/8a)
  • Advanced Pracitioner (Band 8b/8c)
  • Leading Practitioner (Band 8d/9)

Some of our roles may cross job families or role descriptions within the FEDIP framework. The important thing is that we all know what skills we are developing and how we will work in our teams (with line managers and project managers) to do that.

Our welcome process

Before joining, all staff will be sent an induction pack and a copy of one of our favourite books, which covers many of the concepts we believe in.

When joining the team, each new team member can expect a welcome to the ICB, and a welcome to the ICS transformation team. This includes sessions on:

  • Welcome to the ICB (expenses, benefits, HR processes, etc.)
  • Who's who in the system? (our partners, key contacts)
  • How do I get things done? (governance, finance, procurement)
  • Patient-centred digital transformation [with system partners] - our introductory course on user-centred design skills
  • Data into action [with system partners] - our introductory course on using BOB's tools to do data-driven transformation

We make sure all new joiners have a buddy, who is a team member of a similar level, who can help them with questions and help them settle in.

Our development offer

We develop personally, and together as a team. As a rule we believe in spending 80% of time learning on projects, 15% learning from the wider team and 5% on formal learning (though we encourage apprenticeships and postgraduate education).

Directorate

We join the whole directorate fortnightly for a town hall and quarterly for an in-person development away day.

Team

We work in two teams, our project teams and together as a whole DDaT transformation team.

We come together bi-monthly as a whole team for a protected L&D day. This is where we showcase the work the team has been doing and allow time for smaller sessions and 1:1 catch-ups.

In our project teams we recommend a fortnightly retrospective and wellbeing check-in (taking whichever form the team prefers!). This should also cover reflections and learning on the project.

In the Oxford office, we provide a library of books we have enjoyed as a team. If there's a title we haven't got access to, we encourage team members to put in a request and we will order a copy. This includes:

  • Invisible Women - Caroline Criado Perez

As part of our bi-monthly in-person team development day, we set aside time for our team members to talk each other 1:1.

We actively seek out opportunities for our team members to shadow teams inside ICS organisations as well as elsewhere in the ICB because we know it's important to understand how other teams work and what they do. This leads to better digital transformation!

We support our team members to mentor and be mentored, whether this is within our team, or more widely.

Personal - Formal

The ICB provides access to NHS Elect which provides a range of free training in cross-cutting skills. We supplement this with access to the NHS Digital Learning Solutions platform, which provides training in basic and intermediate office skills.

We take the following principles when it comes to the various formal courses that are available:

  • The list below contains some of the vast array of training that is available - we encourage team members to find what's right for them and we can add it to the list later.
  • Just because a course is aimed at one job family, it doesn't mean it isn't useful or allowed for others - e.g. if you're a project manager who wants to learn more about data analysis we encourage this!
  • Where courses require funding (or significant time commitment like apprenticeships) we consider these on a case-by-case basis, prioritising those who don't already have a qualification of that level, or in that area.

Cross-cutting (behaviours, leadership, cross-cutting knowledge)

In line with domain-specific skills, we provide and encourage our team to take up opportunities to become (and improve as) leaders in digital health and care.

Associate Practitioner
  • National Edward Jenner Programme - free, self-guided course designed for anyone aspiring to their first leadership role within the next 1-2 years. Aims to develop essential leadership skills.
Practitioner
  • National £ Mary Seacole Programme - 6-month programme aimed at first-time leaders in healthcare covering leadership fundamentals, leading for improvement and management skills.
  • NHS Elect Mentoring - free, 4 hour course for line managers aimed at helping people develop as a mentor.
Senior Practitioner
  • National PGDip in Digital Health Leadership (Level 7) - centrally funded, 12-month national programme to build digital change leaders resulting in a PGDip (Level 7) in Digital Health Leadership.
  • National £ Rosalind Franklin Programme - 9-month national programme to suport mid-level leaders to become outstanding innovators, leaders, and team members working across the health and care system
  • Appenticeship MSc in Healthcare Leadership (with Elizabeth Garrett Anderson programme) (Level 7) - masters degree apprenticeship programme designed for those aspiring to take on a more senior leadership role and make a wider impact
Advanced Practitioner
  • National £ Nye Bevan Programme - aimed at those aspiring to move into a board role within the next two years, supporting senior leaders to move beyond leadership within their area of expertise

Data

Associate Practitioner
  • National Population Health Management - self-guided online course to support analysts and other health and care professionals working in population health. Covers what PHM is, some of the key approaches and what data sets are available to support it.
  • Appenticeship Data Analyst (Level 4) - 18-month programme empowering participants to develop the skills needed to collect, organise and study data to provide valuable business insight.
  • Appenticeship Data Engineer (Level 5) - 21-month course giving participants skills to build systems which collect, manage, and convert data into valuable information for data scientists/analysts to interpret and translate into business impact.
  • Appenticeship BSc (Hons) Digital & Technology Solutions (Data Analyst) (Level 6) - 42-month degree apprenticeship combining technical foundations with specialist modules on data analysis to become a data scientist or data analyst.
Senior Practitioner
  • Appenticeship MSc Digital and Technology Specialist (Data and Analytics) (Level 7) - 25-month programme aimed at developing advanced technical and leadership skills applicable to all advanced Data and Analytics roles.

Product and Delivery

Associate Practitioner

  • Appenticeship Digital Product Manager (Level 4) - 18-month programme equipping learners with the skills and knowledge they need to become a successful Product Manager.

Associate Practitioner

Our behaviours

No matter which job family or framework our roles are aligned to we make sure we behave in line with the BOB values, namely:

  • Respectful - we embed inclusivity and safe spaces
  • Impactful - we make a difference
  • Integrity - we are kind and fair
  • Leadership - we encourage leadership
  • Collaborative - we work in a positive way

The full list of behaviours can be found on the ICB staff zone [link restricted to ICB employees] and forms part of our formal appraisal process.
